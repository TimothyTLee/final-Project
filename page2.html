Faucet Repair

Faucet Repairs and Easy Drain Clearing

Overview

Lets get started by debunking a myth that faucets are too hard and complicated to attempt repairs. Nothing is further from the truth. All faucets work the same way. You have a cold inlet (right) and a hot inlet (left). Each side has a valve of a specific design that when turned off will not allow water to flow through the faucet mixer portion. these valves are know by a couple of names. Either stems or cartridges. When a handle is turned on the coorsponding water for that side will flow (hot or cold). Now, there are some faucets which are considered "throw away faucets" These are usually cheaply made with plastic materials and cost very little. My advice when purchasing this type of faucet is to be prepared for a faucet that will last anywhere from 1 month to 9 months before it fails. When purchasing faucets - ask questions! a few of the most important ones to ask are: - what is the warranty for both finish and working parts? - Are parts readily avaliable? If the answer is yes make sure you look at them and price them many times the parts are very expensive. - Is there a website to the company with working contact information? Then check it out. If the answer is either No or I Don't Know, or Not sure then look for something else.

Faucets with repair parts available will have either stems or cartridges. So lets learn what the differences are. Stems are a compression style stopper. They are usually a multi-turn type meaning rhe handle will turn a number of rotations to open or close. This type hasa rubber material washer on the end of the stems that seals on a raised section in the valve called a seat. In nearly all cases both washer and seat can be easily replaced to help the faucet perform as it should without leaks. Furthermore, the stems are replaceable as well if and when it finally wears out. This is a common mainenance issue with this type of working component. the cost to repair these can be as low as $1.00 to a high of $40.00 on average. Next we have a cartridge. These are usually a quarter turn style stopper. The main material is a smooth ceramic material. These are not repairable, and will need to be repalces when its time. Expect the price of these types to range from approx $15.00 to $80.00 on average. Cartridges life expectancy is about twice what a stems set and washer will last(not the stem itself). But are more negatively affected by hard water.
